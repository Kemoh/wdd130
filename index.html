Freetown

In 1792, the city was refounded by the Sierra Leone Company and became home to over 1,000 Black Loyalists—African Americans who had fought for the British during the American Revolutionary War and were resettled after being freed. They named the settlement "Freetown" to symbolize the hope of freedom. Throughout the 19th century, Freetown became a center of British colonial administration and a hub for education and commerce in West Africa. The influence of the liberated African settlers, known as the Krio, shaped the city's culture and language, which remains significant today.
