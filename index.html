Freetown

Freetown, the capital city of Sierra Leone, is a vibrant coastal hub nestled on the Atlantic Ocean. Known for its rich history, it was founded in the late 18th century as a settlement for freed African American and West Indian slaves. Today, Freetown is a dynamic blend of cultures, bustling markets, colonial architecture, and scenic beaches. Landmarks like the historic Cotton Tree and the Sierra Leone National Museum reflect its storied past, while the city's lively atmosphere and resilient spirit continue to shape its modern identity.
